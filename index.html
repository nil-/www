It's like competitive video gaming... but socially accepted! To be honest I actually prefer Go due to higher game complexity, but no one plays it so now I rarely do either. I've grown to love chess though, particularly because of the larger amount of theory developed in the metagame.


Zero sum games with incomplete information are just so so fun, especially if there's RNG involved. It's an added layer of complexity in trying to reach the highest expected value, and not simply the highest deterministic score.

My playing style tends to vary depending on the table. I usually play fundamental poker (and thus tight aggressive) against fish, and I become more and more loose as the players are more skillful and thus willing to give up pots.

I was 12-tabling at 5/10 NL and making quite a bit of money during senior year of high school and early freshman year in college. Now if only online gambling wasn't still illegal..


I love the following in no particular order: Vim, Colemak, GNU/Linux tiling managers, Vim-keybinded applications, and ultra-lightweight software.

It's so rewarding to create personal programs to accomodate your needs, for example, an automated script to save you time from having to do a menial task on a regular basis. Simple and modular is my philosophy. There's also the aesthetic appeal that I admire about ultra-customizability in Unix.

Unlike most neckbeards, however, I really am not so concerned about the Free Software Movement, user privacy, and elitist programming-to-newbie attitudes, e.g., RTFMP. Not using proprietary software ironically restricts one's freedom; Google is doing more good than evil; closeted societies are worse than Hitler.

You can see my PC stuff in collections/battlestation. I'm also extremely enthusiastic about the latest toys, which you can find in collections/devices.

I enjoy all mediums, although I'm currently most fond of lit and films. I usually abide by western convention—solid character development, presentation, plot progression, and overall message. I do however have a preference for atmosphere more than most, and am also not one to fault, say, eastern works from a western penchant.

I would say my attitude in general leans toward art house works, in visuals, in prose, or in whatever lends the most strength and is most niche to the medium. I find they really challenge the meaning of art and the expression within it.

Looking at my lists, I also seem to enjoy bildungsroman a lot, and am partial to a tinge of cheesiness in my stories (I blame my upbringing for the latter).

You can find all my ratings as well as a list of my favorites on the collections/media page.


Writing happens to be one of my favorite activities because it requires expression from deep-seated feeling. I also find it to be the most valuable time sink: it's a personal accomplishment that you'll always look back and feel proud of. It's your own unique creation. With a scientific discovery, another person in the future could have (and will) find it eventually, but within the humanities you can be proud to say that your own work is precisely that. Too bad I'm not good at it.
